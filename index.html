Urban Intelligence and Analytics

Understanding cities to build better futures

Cities are living, breathing systems. Every day, millions of people make choices about where to live, how to travel, where to work, and how to spend their time. These individual decisions interact with infrastructure, policies, and the environment in complex ways — often producing outcomes no one planned.

Urban Intelligence and Analytics exists to make sense of this complexity.

We use data, computer models, and artificial intelligence to understand how cities work, how they change over time, and how policy decisions can lead to better outcomes for people and places. Our aim is simple: to help decision-makers explore what might happen before choices are locked in.

What do we do?

We build realistic computer simulations of cities and their populations, allowing us to explore how housing, transport, energy, health, and the environment interact. We are currently involved in a number of projects exploring how housing, transport, energy, health, and the environment interact.
